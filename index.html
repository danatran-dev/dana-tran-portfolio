An internal AI agent built for Google's sales teams that enabled advertisers and business owners to generate campaign collateral in seconds. Reps would input the campaign's core purpose, landing page URL, KPIs, and goals — and the agent would output ready-to-use creative and copy assets tailored to the campaign type: video, search, or social.

Sales teams were spending hours manually researching text variants, visual directions, and audience targeting logic for each new advertiser campaign. This created inconsistency across reps, slowed onboarding for new advertisers, and bottlenecked high-volume sales cycles. The gap wasn't product capability — it was translating advertiser intent into campaign-ready outputs fast enough to be useful.

Mapped the full sales rep workflow from initial advertiser brief to campaign submission to identify where manual effort was concentrated. Partnered with engineering to scope the agent. Developed positioning around time savings and output quality rather than "AI" as a feature — framing it as a workflow accelerator, not a replacement. Built internal sales enablement materials, a rep onboarding guide, and a feedback loop to capture edge cases and iterate continuously.

Conducted structured feedback sessions with sales reps across SMB to large enterprise segments to understand how they described campaign goals and what output gaps created rework. Discovered that reps often received vague advertiser briefs and needed the agent to handle ambiguity gracefully. The customer's voice — from rep frustration to advertiser outcome — directly shaped how the agent was positioned and what the onboarding guidance prioritized.

Metis is an agent post-training infrastructure company that helps enterprises take AI agents from prototype to production — reliably, at scale. Most agents break in real-world scenarios: they miss context, fail edge cases, and fall apart when the environment changes. Metis solves this through end-to-end post-training infrastructure (real-world evals, custom environments, observability), frontier reinforcement learning research, and forward-deployed engineers who work hands-on with engineering teams to drive measurable performance improvements.

Metis was preparing for a public launch into a market where the pain was real but the category didn't yet have a name. Engineering teams with agents in production knew their agents were hitting a ceiling — missing context, failing edge cases, degrading over time — but weren't searching for "post-training infrastructure." They were searching for answers. The GTM challenge: tighten the ICP to teams already running production agents, and build a narrative that made buyers feel seen before a single sales conversation happened.

Audited the ICP and rebuilt it tightly around enterprises with agents already in production — companies where reliability failures had real revenue consequences. Repositioned Metis away from "evaluation tooling" toward the more accurate and resonant frame: post-training infrastructure that takes agents to the frontier. Developed a full GTM blueprint covering positioning, problem framing, JTBD, and success metrics. Built a three-tiered outreach framework matched to distinct buyer pain patterns, then wrote the sales script, objection handling guide, and FAQ to reduce improvisation across the team.

Ran qualitative discovery calls with potential buyers to pressure-test ICP and messaging — letting the voice of the market reshape the narrative. Buyers consistently described the problem as their agents "hitting a ceiling" in production: they'd work in demos but fall apart in real-world scenarios. This language became the anchor for Metis's positioning. The research also surfaced a critical disqualifier: companies still in the prototype stage weren't ready buyers, and pursuing them was diluting the pipeline with conversations that couldn't close.

Circle is the issuer of USDC, one of the world's largest dollar-backed stablecoins, operating a suite of financial infrastructure products used by developers, fintechs, and enterprises to move money on-chain. The GTM motion required coordinating across highly technical engineering teams, a sales org closing enterprise partnerships, and a marketing team positioning a complex financial product to a broad, fragmented buyer landscape.

When joining the PMM team, the GTM operation was producing duplicative briefs across functions — marketing had its own positioning, sales had its own narratives, and engineering was shipping features without a shared framework for communicating value externally. Campaigns launched without proper sales enablement, collateral didn't reflect current product capabilities, and use case documentation wasn't mapped to buyer verticals.

Audited the existing GTM brief library and identified where redundancy was introducing misalignment. Consolidated the architecture into a single brief format, rebuilt messaging to be outcome-focused and vertical-specific, and developed 20+ use cases across payments, fintech infrastructure, enterprise treasury, and developer tooling — structured for publication to drive inbound qualification and reduce sales cycle friction.

Embedded with sales and engineering leads to hear directly from the market. Sales reps surfaced gaps around developer integration complexity and enterprise compliance that collateral wasn't addressing. Engineering flagged where external messaging was outpacing real capabilities, creating expectation mismatches in pilots. Both sets of insights were integrated into the messaging framework and a technical sign-off process for every brief before external deployment.

Hook'd is a fashion and culture publication founded from scratch at UT Austin and scaled into a multi-channel media brand with 200+ active contributors, six 100+ page print and digital publications, and a community that generated 300K+ TikTok views and 3K Instagram followers within the first year. Before it was any of those things, it was a positioning insight: there was no media platform built for aspiring creatives who didn't have a portfolio yet.

The existing landscape of fashion and culture media was aspirational to the point of exclusion — dominated by publications that required experience, credentials, or a polished body of work before you could participate. For university students trying to break into creative industries, this created a catch-22: you couldn't build a portfolio without opportunities, but you couldn't get opportunities without a portfolio.

Defined the core value proposition as "the publication where you build the portfolio, not the one that requires it," and built all external messaging, recruitment, and partnership outreach around that single idea. Led end-to-end production cycles for six publications within 4-month windows. Secured partnerships with 10+ beauty brands and 24 industry professionals — positioning Hook'd as a career launchpad, creating a self-reinforcing growth loop where contributor outcomes became the brand's most powerful proof point.

The clearest validation came from how contributors talked about Hook'd unprompted — in job interviews, on LinkedIn, in conversations with recruiters. They didn't say "I write for a student magazine." They said "I was on the editorial team at Hook'd" and listed it as a professional credential. That behavioral signal proved the positioning was working and reshaped the brand's external narrative — centering real contributor outcomes as the core proof, not the publication itself.

From Walk-Ins to a Repeatable Engine — Paid Ads, Automation, and Retention Built from Zero

Plaza Aesthetics is a medical spa in a highly competitive, appointment-driven market where client trust, experience quality, and post-visit follow-through are the primary drivers of both retention and word-of-mouth growth. When joining the operation, there was no existing marketing infrastructure — no paid acquisition strategy, no automated client communication, and no structured retention program. Everything needed to be built from zero.

The business was operating without a repeatable system for attracting new clients or keeping existing ones. New customer acquisition relied entirely on walk-ins and organic referrals with no paid amplification. On the retention side, clients who had positive experiences were not being re-engaged after their visit — no follow-up cadence, no appointment reminders, and no mechanism to turn satisfied clients into active referrers.

Audited the full client journey from first touchpoint to post-visit to identify where drop-off was occurring. Built a paid acquisition strategy across Google and Meta Ads with treatment-specific campaigns and geo-targeting. Simultaneously redesigned the operational infrastructure: reconfigured scheduling software, automated payments and inventory management, and built a post-visit workflow including automated follow-up messaging, appointment reminders, and a referral program built from scratch.

Spent time observing the in-person experience and speaking directly with staff and returning clients to understand what drove people to come back — and what caused them not to. The clearest finding: clients who felt remembered returned. Those who received no communication after their visit didn't. This customer insight anchored the entire retention strategy around relationship over transaction — shaping the tone, timing, and content of every automated touchpoint in the post-visit workflow.

Plaza Aesthetics went from no marketing infrastructure to a functioning, automated growth and retention engine — built in under six months. The project demonstrated that customer empathy isn't just a research exercise: it's an operational principle. Understanding that clients needed to feel remembered directly determined which systems got built. The referral program proved that when the experience is strong and the follow-up is thoughtful, your existing customer base becomes your most efficient acquisition channel.
